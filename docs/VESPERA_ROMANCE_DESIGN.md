# Vespera Thorne: Narrative Bond & "Romance" Design

> **NOTA:** Leer, revisar y aplicar cambios necesarios.

This document outlines the narrative design for the bond arc with **Vespera 'Gutter-Rot' Thorne**. Due to her background of extreme trauma, trafficking, and haphephobia (fear of touch), this arc **must not** follow standard RPG romance tropes.

## Core Concept: "Two Knives in the Dark"
The arc is not about "fixing" or "saving" her. It is about **autonomy** and **redefining safety**. It is a Gothic, psychological journey where the player proves they are the only person who does not view her as a product or a tool.

---

## I. The Four Pillars of Design

1.  **Radical Autonomy:** The player must **never** initiate physical contact. Vespera must have absolute control over when and how she is touched.
2.  **De-Transactionalization:** Vespera believes all relationships are transactional (pain/pleasure for safety/food). The player must consistently prove they want nothing from her in exchange for their aid.
3.  **The Beauty of the Grotesque:** She mutilated herself to be repulsive. The player must accept her "monstrous" nature and rage as valid, rather than trying to heal her scars or make her "pretty" again.
4.  **Non-Sexual Intimacy:** Sex is a trigger. Intimacy is redefined as safety: sleeping in the same room without fear, sharing silence, or allowing a wound to be bandaged.

---

## II. Narrative Phases (The Affinity Arc)

### Phase 1: The Monster Test (Low Affinity)
*   **Behavior:** Vespera is actively repulsive, rude, and describes her trauma graphically to disgust the player.
*   **The Trap:** She may coldly offer herself sexually ("If you want payment, make it quick").
*   **Required Player Action:** **Reject the offer firmly but without judgment.**
    *   *Example Dialogue:* "I want nothing from you that isn't given freely. And right now, you have nothing to give."
*   **Result:** Confusion. Breaks her transactional worldview.

### Phase 2: Silent Observation (Medium Affinity)
*   **Behavior:** Insults stop. She watches the player constantly, studying how they treat the weak.
*   **Key Event (The Wound):** Vespera is injured.
    *   *Fail State:* Trying to use magic/touch without asking.
    *   *Success State:* Placing supplies on the ground and stepping back. "You are bleeding. Here are bandages. I will stand guard while you tend to it."
*   **Result:** Recognition of bodily autonomy.

### Phase 3: Trench Intimacy (High Affinity)
*   **The Gift:** Instead of flowers, she shares something grotesque. E.g., Offering a vial of "Milk of the Poppy" when the player is in pain, or showing a finger-bone trophy and telling its story.
*   **The Sleep Scene:** During a Long Rest, Vespera sleeps near the fire with her back to the player.
    *   *Symbolism:* Exposing her back is the ultimate sign of trust for prey.

### Phase 4: The Choice (Max Affinity / "Romance")
*   **The Climax:** She admits the silence in her head isn't scary when you are there.
*   **Key Dialogue:** "The world is a slaughterhouse. Always will be. But... you aren't holding the knife. You're the only one not holding a knife."
*   **The Breakthrough:** **She initiates contact.** A brush of a hand (perhaps armored), or leaning against the player's shoulder.
*   **Outcome:** Not a "cure," but a partnership. Two broken things facing the dark together.

---

## III. Proposed Mechanics

### 1. The "Touch Trigger"
*   **Mechanic:** If the player selects a dialogue option involving non-consensual touch (e.g., `[Hug her]`, `[Wipe tears]`), Affinity immediately drops to **-50** or initiates combat.
*   **UI Warning:** These options should perhaps not be marked as "Evil," trapping players who try to be "generic nice heroes" without listening to her trauma.

### 2. Autonomy Score
*   Hidden variable. Increases when the player asks "What do you want to do?" instead of issuing commands.

### 3. "No-Action" Choices
*   In conversations with Vespera, the "Correct" answer should often be silence or inaction.
    *   *Option:* `[Sit in silence and watch the fire]` vs `[Try to comfort her with words]`.

## V. Post-Arc Evolution: "The Shield in the Dark"

If the player successfully navigates the bond arc, Vespera does not become "soft," but she stops being volatile. She transitions from a Survivor to a Protector.

### 1. The Shift in Philosophy
*   **Old View:** "I must hurt everyone before they hurt me."
*   **New View:** "The world is dark, but I will be the monster that stands in the dark so you can sleep."
*   **Role:** She becomes the **Silent Guardian**. She is still lethal, but her rage is focused solely on oppressors (slavers, abusers, The Gilded Cage), not the world at large.

### 2. The Paradox of Recovery (The "Body" Conflict)
*   **The Event:** As Vespera eats regularly and stops using heavy drugs, her body recovers. She stops being skeletal. Because of her high physical stats (DEX/CON), she naturally develops an athletic, curvy, and objectively "attractive" physique.
*   **The New Trauma:** This terrifies her. To her, looking healthy/beautiful means she is becoming a "product" again. She feels exposed.
*   **Visual Change:** She begins wearing heavy cloaks, baggy gear, or full-body armor to hide her returning form.
*   **Resolution:** The player must validate her body as a **weapon**, not an ornament.
    *   *Wrong:* "You look beautiful/healthy." (Trigger: She feels watched).
    *   *Right:* "You look dangerous/strong." (Validation: She feels powerful).
*   **Outcome:** She accepts her physical restoration not as a return to being a victim, but as forging a stronger vessel for her vengeance.

### 3. Tangible Changes (Visual & Mechanical)
*   **Identity:** Name change to **"Vespera Thorne"**.
*   **Appearance:**
    *   Removes barbed wire (she trusts you not to grab her).
    *   Replaces "gutter trash" armor with professional, maintained dark leather/metal.
    *   Scars heal from red/weeping to white/dry.
*   **Resting Mechanic:** Description changes from "Vespera watches the shadows" to "Vespera sleeps deeply."
